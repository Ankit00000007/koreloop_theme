    TOKEN

    A crypto token is a digital asset that is created, issued, and managed on a blockchain. Unlike coins (such as Bitcoin or Ethereum), tokens do not have their own blockchain but operate on existing blockchains like Ethereum, Binance Smart Chain (BSC), Solana, or Polygon.

    Key Characteristics of Crypto Tokens

    Built on Existing Blockchains

    Tokens rely on blockchains like Ethereum (ERC-20), Binance Smart Chain (BEP-20), or Solana (SPL).

    Smart Contract Based

    Tokens are governed by smart contracts, which automate transfers, staking, and rewards.

    Used for Transactions & Rewards

    Tokens can be used for staking, governance, payments, and DeFi applications.

    Can Be Transferred & Traded

    Tokens can be listed on exchanges and traded like cryptocurrencies.

    AUTOPOOL

    Key Features of AutoPool

    AutoPool typically refers to an automated staking or investment pool system designed to distribute rewards in a structured or cyclical manner. It is commonly used in staking platforms, matrix systems, smart contracts, and MLM-based crypto projects.

    Automated Earnings Distribution

    AutoPool mechanisms automatically distribute rewards based on predefined conditions, eliminating the need for manual intervention.

    Smart Contract-Based

    Most AutoPool systems operate on blockchain smart contracts, ensuring transparency, security, and decentralization.

    Cyclical or Tiered Structure

    Participants often move through multiple levels, cycles, or matrices, earning as they progress.

    Passive Income Generation

    Users stake, invest, or buy positions in the AutoPool, and the system allocates rewards over time.
